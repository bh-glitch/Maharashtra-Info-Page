Its capital is Mumbai, which is the financial and commercial hub of the country. The most commonly spoken language is Marathi and Hindi. History Of Maharastra:

Before India gained Independence, Maharashtra was ruled by the Satavahana dynasty, Deccan sultanates, Mughals, Marathas and British. The British gained total control of the state after the Third Anglo-Maratha War, where they defeated Peshwa Bajirao II. The British ruled for more than a century and brought along immense changes in the lifestyle of the people living in Maharashtra region. After Indian Independence was gained, the princely states and Jagirs of the Deccan States Agency were merged into Bombay State.

Food Culture: :

Maharashtra cuisine covers different vivid tastes from mild-medium-and very spicy dishes. Authentic Mahrashtrian cover staple foods like wheat, rice, jowar, lentils. Some famous dishes that are known for their mouth-watering taste are Pav Bhaji, Vada Pav, Missal Pav, Puran Poli, and many more such. Main meals like lunch and dinner are served in an authentic manner in a plate called thali. Being so close to the sea, seafood is among the popular in the cuisine. The most popular fish is Bombil or commonly known as the Bombay duck

Culture of People:

Most people here speak the native language Marathi but the most common spoken language is Hindi, with presence of so many people from different states, the state can be called a multi-lingual state. When it comes to attire, traditionally women wore and still wear the attested nine-yard long sari, but with the modernization we often find women and girls dressed in western outfits. On the other hand among men the western dressing has a greater acceptance but on occasions the traditional dhoti and pheta, commonly known as kurta is worn by them. The Gandhi cap is the popular apparel in rural Maharashtra.
